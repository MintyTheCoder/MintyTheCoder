## `01` &nbsp;About Me

Started in computer science with game development, building and shipping three Unity games and a VR project for high school classes and extracurricular activities. This evolved into a fascination with the systems that make them possible. Working with Arduino projects introduced me to embedded systems and gave me a passionate interest for the relationship between hardware and software.

My high school experience is what led me to pursue a dual degree in Computer Engineering, with a concentration in Chip-Scale Integration, and Computer Science. I am especially interested in the hardware-software boundary, where architecture, embedded systems, and low-level optimization come together. I enjoy understanding not only how software works, but also how the hardware beneath it is designed.

My long-term goal is to contribute to semiconductor and computer hardware research, helping design the next generation of computing systems that are faster, more efficient, and more capable. I am always looking for opportunities to build projects that deepen my understanding of computer systems.
